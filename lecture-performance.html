Contemporary art often exists between presentation, research, and narration. Lecture performance brings these forms together as a spatial essay in which images, spoken material, and staged fragments remain visibly connected.

The project follows architectures of learning and display: rooms, projection surfaces, notes, and image sequences become a score for a live situation. Instead of separating artwork and mediation, the format keeps both in motion.
